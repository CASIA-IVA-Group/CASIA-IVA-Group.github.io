Abstract

An intelligent system is required to have omni-perceptual capabilities from multiple modalities including vision, text, and audio. To this end, this paper establishes an \textbf{O}mni-\textbf{P}erception pre-\textbf{T}raining Benchmark(OPT) aiming at promoting the researches about multi-modal understanding and generation through pretraining techniques. A million-level tri-modality benchmark dataset as OPTSet is first collected by manually annotating video with high-quality descriptions from the auditory and visual perspectives. Such a vision-text-audio correlated dataset spawns three new tri-modality tasks, namely video\&audio retrieval, caption and generation, which are explored together with existing dual-modality tasks to evaluate the performance of an Omni-Perception pre-Training model comprehensively. Furthermore, we design an end-to-end tri-modality pretraining model with raw video, sentences, and audio as inputs, which consists of three separate encoders, a multi-modal encoder, a single multi-modal decoder. Dedicated pretext tasks are designed to achieve multi-modal understanding and generation capability across three modalities, and a modality erasing strategy is adopted to strengthen dual-modality performance. Under OPT benchmark, we achieve state-of-the-arts performances on various downstream tasks including video retrieval, video caption, video QA, audio retrieval and audio caption.
